PRESSURE

Pressure is defined as force per unit area
P=F/A
where F is the Force, A is Area and P is Pressure.
From the above expression,a large force on a small area produces a high pressure and a small force on a large area produces a low pressure.
Therefore,pressure increases as force increases and area decreases.

Example:A plastic block weighing 25N has dimensions 34cm by 25cm by 10cm. What is the greatest pressure and the least pressure it can exert on the ground?

Solution: Force=25N
dimensions=0.34x0.25x0.1
Surface Area A1=0.34x0.25=0.085m2
Pressure P1=25/0.085=294Pa Surface Area A2=0.25x0.1=0.025m2
Pressure P2=25/0.025=1000Pa
Surface Area A3=0.34x0.1=0.034m2
